How to Build Your Perfect Routine

Expert tips so you never waste money on the wrong product

1

Know Your Skin Type First

Oily, dry, combination, or sensitive — every product choice starts here. If you're unsure, a dermatologist visit or even a simple blotting-paper test can save you hundreds in trial-and-error purchases.

2

Layer Products Thinnest to Thickest

Apply serums before creams and always finish with SPF in the morning. This ensures each product absorbs properly instead of sitting on top of the last one.

3

Introduce One New Product at a Time

Wait at least two weeks before adding another new product. This way you'll know exactly what's working — and what's causing a breakout.

4

Don't Sleep on SPF

Sunscreen is the single most effective anti-aging product you can use. A lightweight formula like Supergoop! Unseen Sunscreen makes it easy to wear daily without the white cast.

5

Invest in Tools, Save on Basics

Splurge on items with real technology differences — like the Dyson Airwrap — but don't overpay for simple formulas. Brands like CeraVe and The Ordinary deliver clinical results at drugstore prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most

How do I know which products are right for my skin type?
Start with our Buying Guide above and filter by your skin concern. If you have sensitive skin, look for fragrance-free options like CeraVe. For oily or acne-prone skin, The Ordinary Niacinamide and Paula's Choice BHA are excellent starting points.
Are these products authentic and not counterfeit?
Every product we feature links directly to authorized retailers and official brand stores. We never source from third-party gray-market sellers, so you can shop with confidence.
Is the Dyson Airwrap really worth the price?
For anyone who styles their hair regularly, yes. It replaces multiple hot tools, causes significantly less heat damage, and cuts styling time roughly in half. Think of it as a long-term investment in healthier hair.
Can I use The Ordinary Niacinamide with Vitamin C?
Yes — despite an old internet myth, niacinamide and vitamin C work well together. Just apply the thinner serum first, let it absorb for a minute, then layer the next one on top.
How often should I exfoliate with Paula's Choice BHA?
Start with 2-3 times per week and see how your skin responds. Most people can work up to daily use, but if you notice any irritation or dryness, scale back and make sure you're using SPF during the day.
